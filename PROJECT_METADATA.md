##### Background

I was born into an ordinary Chinese family of modest means. During my childhood and adolescence, reading was one of the few pursuits that neither my family nor the broader social environment regarded as an unproductive distraction. As a result, I developed a lifelong habit of reading and gradually found great enjoyment in critical learning and independent reflection.

When I entered university, I chose to study mathematics. My university campus was located on the outskirts of a major city, and throughout my undergraduate years I frequently traveled to remote towns and rural areas nearby. These experiences greatly deepened my understanding of the realities of Chinese society. Combined with my extensive reading, they inspired numerous reflections, many of which were recorded as scattered diary entries.

In the autumn of 2022, I experienced an unexpected breakthrough while contemplating what I regarded as one of the most decisive questions in Chinese history: **why did the historical trajectory of China diverge so fundamentally from that of the rest of the world, especially the Western world?** As I pursued this question further, I found that it resembled the loose end of a tangled skein of yarn. Once that single thread was pulled free, the broader process of human historical development gradually unfolded with remarkable clarity. In retrospect, the most intellectually demanding part of the entire manuscript was completed during the following two years.

By the end of 2024, I decided to transform these previously scattered notes into a coherent manuscript. The first complete Chinese edition was finished in mid-March 2025, containing approximately 420,000 Chinese characters. During the following year, the manuscript continued to expand through substantial revisions and additions. In May 2026, I completed its concluding postscript, bringing the finalized Chinese edition to approximately 450,000 Chinese characters. During the same month, I also completed an independent companion essay entitled *Thoughts on the Future*.

Over the following two months, I translated the manuscript into English. By early July 2026, two English editions had been completed: a faithful translation corresponding closely to the Chinese original, and a **Refined and Guided Edition**, in which introductory notes and structural guidance were added to facilitate reading for an international audience.

### Additional Description:

##### Abstract

This work represents the principal outcome of an independent, interdisciplinary study conducted from the perspective of comparative history. It is fundamentally a work of history and sociology. Taking the well-known "Great Divergence" between East and West as its point of departure, the study investigates why China's historical trajectory diverged so profoundly from that of the rest of the world. Moving beyond the limitations of conventional explanations such as cultural determinism and geographical determinism, the research builds upon established scholarship while integrating a wide range of academic theories. By introducing the concept of the transition of the ***primary factor of production***, a concept proposed in this study, as the central criterion for distinguishing stages of social evolution, the analysis extends beyond China to encompass the historical development of human civilizations as a whole. It seeks to explain why different civilizations, despite confronting many similar political, economic, and cultural challenges, followed fundamentally different historical paths. The result is a unified theoretical framework for explaining historical transformation on a global scale.

The study argues that, following the end of the Classical Age, China experienced an abnormal interruption in its process of feudalization, allowing the restoration of the highly centralized ***bureaucratic state***. This historical development constituted the underlying source of many of China's subsequent social characteristics. Through systematic comparisons between China, Europe, Japan, the Middle East, and other regions, the manuscript offers a broader analytical perspective for interdisciplinary studies in global history and reexamines the origins of modern civilization and the fundamental mechanisms driving its development.

The research emphasizes mechanism-oriented explanation rather than descriptive narrative and seeks, wherever possible, to formulate historically falsifiable propositions. It is not intended as the final conclusion on the development of human history, but rather as an open theoretical framework designed to encourage further discussion, critical examination, and interdisciplinary research.

##### Reading Recommendations

Given the breadth of the subjects covered in this manuscript, the high density of its theoretical innovations, and the fact that the study begins with Chinese history and frequently draws upon Classical Chinese historical sources, the work may be challenging for first-time readers.

The author therefore recommends beginning with the **Refined and Guided Edition** of the English manuscript, which contains sectional introductions designed to facilitate understanding of the overall structure and arguments.

Before reading the main body of the manuscript, readers are also encouraged to read **Section 30 of Chapter 12** in its entirety. In that section, the author provides necessary explanations of more than one hundred concepts that may otherwise cause confusion throughout the manuscript.

Readers may further benefit from using AI-assisted reading tools to gain a comprehensive understanding of the manuscript's structure, conceptual framework, and overall arguments. **NotebookLM** is particularly recommended for this purpose.
